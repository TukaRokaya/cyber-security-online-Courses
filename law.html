Cyber Laws

Understand the legal framework protecting digital space and online rights

What are Cyber Laws?

Cyber laws are legal measures that govern activities on the internet and digital devices. These laws protect users, organizations, and governments from cyber crimes such as hacking, identity theft, online fraud, and data breaches.

Important Cyber Laws Topics

  • Data Protection: Laws that ensure personal and sensitive data are stored and processed securely.
  • Privacy: Protecting the right of individuals to control their personal information online.
  • Cyber Crime: Legal provisions against hacking, phishing, malware distribution, and cyber terrorism.
  • Intellectual Property Rights: Protecting digital content like software, music, and videos from unauthorized use.
  • Electronic Contracts and Signatures: Legal recognition of digital agreements and signatures.

Examples of Cyber Crimes

Cyber crimes include unauthorized access to computer systems, spreading viruses, identity theft, cyberbullying, and online scams.

Why are Cyber Laws Important?

With the increasing reliance on digital technology, cyber laws help maintain trust and security online. They deter criminals, provide remedies to victims, and encourage safe use of the internet and digital tools.
